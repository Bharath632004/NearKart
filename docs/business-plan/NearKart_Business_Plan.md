# CHAPTER 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

## 1.1 Business Overview

NearKart is a hyperlocal delivery platform that connects customers with nearby grocery stores, supermarkets, pharmacies, and local merchants for same-day and ultra-fast delivery within a 5–10 km radius. Unlike national e-commerce giants, NearKart focuses on last-mile speed, local inventory availability, and supporting small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs).

NearKart's platform consists of:
- A **Customer App** (Android & iOS) for browsing, ordering, and tracking
- A **Merchant Portal** (Web & Mobile) for inventory, order, and analytics management
- A **Delivery Partner App** (Android) for delivery assignment and navigation
- An **Admin Dashboard** for platform-wide operations and analytics

## 1.2 The Opportunity

India's hyperlocal delivery market is one of the fastest-growing segments in the country's tech-driven economy. With over 650 million smartphone users, widespread UPI adoption, and a deep-rooted habit of shopping from local stores, the demand for a technology bridge between neighborhood merchants and customers has never been stronger.

The COVID-19 pandemic permanently accelerated online ordering behavior even in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities. Consumers now expect:
- Orders fulfilled within 30–60 minutes
- Real-time GPS tracking
- Competitive pricing compared to large platforms
- Contactless payment

Local merchants, on the other hand, lack the digital tools to compete with organized players. NearKart fills this gap.

## 1.3 Business Model

NearKart operates on a **commission-based + SaaS subscription hybrid model**:
- **Commission:** 8–15% per order from merchants
- **Delivery Fees:** Charged to customers (₹15–₹40 per order)
- **Merchant Subscription Plans:** ₹499–₹2,499/month for premium features
- **Advertising Revenue:** Promoted product placements and banner ads
- **White-label Licensing:** Platform licensing for regional partners

# CHAPTER 4: PROBLEM STATEMENT

## 4.1 The Core Problem

Despite India having over 12 million neighborhood kirana stores and local merchants, the majority of them remain invisible to the digital consumer. Simultaneously, customers in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities cannot access the same quality of quick delivery that metro residents enjoy.

## 4.2 Pain Points – Customers

| Pain Point | Severity | Current Workaround |
|------------|----------|-------------------|
| Long wait times (2–3 days) from national platforms | High | None |
| No real-time inventory visibility of local stores | High | Phone calls to shops |
| No trusted delivery tracking system | High | Uncertainty & anxiety |
| Limited payment options at local shops | Medium | Cash dependency |
| Inability to compare prices across nearby stores | Medium | Manual visiting |
| Language barriers on existing platforms | Medium | Avoid platforms |

## 4.3 Pain Points – Merchants

| Pain Point | Severity | Impact |
|------------|----------|--------|
| No digital storefront | Very High | Customer loss to organized retail |
| No order management system | High | Manual errors, missed orders |
| No delivery infrastructure | High | Cannot offer home delivery |
| No analytics or insights | Medium | Blind business decisions |
| High commission rates from existing platforms | High | Thin margins |
| Complex onboarding on existing platforms | High | Digital exclusion |

## 4.4 Pain Points – Delivery Partners

| Pain Point | Severity |
|------------|----------|
| No stable income or order assignment fairness | High |
| No in-app navigation optimized for local lanes | Medium |
| No incentive or earnings visibility | Medium |

## 4.5 Market Failure

Existing solutions like Swiggy Instamart, Blinkit, and Zepto primarily target metro cities (Tier 1) with a dark store model that excludes local merchants entirely. Amazon, Flipkart, and Meesho focus on national logistics, making same-day hyperlocal delivery impossible. NearKart uniquely solves the **last-mile, neighborhood-level** delivery gap.

India's quick commerce and hyperlocal delivery sector is undergoing explosive growth. The sector was valued at approximately $3.34 billion in 2023 and is projected to reach $12.14 billion by 2028, growing at a CAGR of 29.4%.

**Key Market Drivers:**
- 65% of India's population is under 35 years old (digital-native consumer base)
- UPI transactions crossed 15 billion/month in 2024
- 8x growth in smartphone usage in Tier 2/3 cities since 2019
- Post-pandemic behavioral shift: 72% of Indian consumers now prefer online grocery ordering
- Government's Digital India initiative accelerating merchant digitization

# CHAPTER 11: REVENUE MODEL

## 11.1 Revenue Streams

### Stream 1: Transaction Commission
- **Source:** Charged to merchants per completed order
- **Rate:** 8% – 15% of order value (tiered by category)
- **Example:** ₹500 grocery order → ₹40–₹75 commission

| Merchant Category | Commission Rate |
|------------------|----------------|
| Grocery & Kirana | 8% |
| Pharmacy | 10% |

| Restaurant / Food | 15% |

- **Source:** Charged to customer per order
- **Rate:** ₹15 – ₹40 based on distance and time
- **Free delivery threshold:** ₹299+ order value

|----------|-------------|
| 0–2 km | ₹15 |

| 5–8 km | ₹35 |

4. **Training** – Video tutorial + WhatsApp support (1 hour)
5. **Go Live** – Store published on platform

**Target:** Merchant onboarded and live within **48 hours** of registration.
